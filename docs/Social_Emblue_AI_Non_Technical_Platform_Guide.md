# Social Emblue AI

## Non-Technical Platform Guide

Social Emblue AI is a hosted platform that helps companies monitor online conversations, understand customer behavior, respond faster, and measure the impact of their social media activity.

Companies use the platform through a secure web dashboard. They do not receive, own, or access the source code. They are paying for access to the service, not for ownership of the software.

---

## 1. What The Platform Does

Social Emblue AI helps companies answer important questions such as:

- What are people saying about our brand?
- What are people saying about our competitors?
- Who is complaining, asking questions, or showing buying interest?
- Which conversations need urgent attention?
- What content ideas can we get from real customer language?
- Which campaign replies, links, and conversations are producing results?
- Are there early signs of a crisis?

The platform combines social listening, AI analysis, reply assistance, campaign engagement, and reporting.

In simple terms:

> Social Emblue AI finds important conversations online, explains what they mean, helps teams respond, and measures the outcome.

---

## 2. How Companies Use The System

Each company gets its own private workspace.

Inside that workspace, the company can:

- Add its brand profile
- Set its tone of voice
- Add keywords to monitor
- Connect social media accounts
- Run historical searches
- View important conversations
- Generate reply suggestions
- Monitor campaign activity
- Track clicks and conversions
- Download reports

Each company only sees its own data.

They do not see other clients, other campaigns, backend systems, AI prompts, private infrastructure, or source code.

---

## 3. Client Onboarding Workflow

The usual onboarding process looks like this:

1. A client is added to the platform.
2. A brand workspace is created for them.
3. Their brand name, industry, campaign goals, and tone of voice are added.
4. Their important keywords are configured.
5. Their social accounts are connected.
6. Their team members are invited.
7. The system starts monitoring conversations and campaign activity.

Example setup:

**Brand:** Access Bank  
**Tone:** Professional, helpful, trustworthy  
**Goal:** Find frustrated competitor customers and improve response speed  
**Keywords:** Failed transaction, bank app not working, GTBank complaint, switch banks  
**Platforms:** X, Reddit, YouTube, Instagram, TikTok

## 4. Social Listening Workflow

Social listening is the core of the platform.

It helps a company monitor public conversations around selected keywords.

For example, a bank may monitor:

- Competitor complaints
- Customer service issues
- Buying signals
- Crisis keywords
- Product category terms

The client creates keyword groups such as:

- Competitor Complaints
- Crisis Watch
- Product Interest
- Brand Mentions
- Industry Trends
- Customer Pain Points

Once a keyword group is active, the system checks for new public conversations regularly.

When a relevant conversation is found, the system organizes it by:

- Platform
- Keyword matched
- Sentiment
- Intent
- Urgency
- Author
- Date
- Engagement level

The most important conversations appear first.

---

## 5. Real-Time Monitoring Workflow

Real-time monitoring is used for ongoing surveillance.

Example:

A customer posts:

> “GTBank failed my transfer again. I need another bank.”

The system understands this as:

- Negative sentiment
- Possible switching intent
- High urgency
- Opportunity for another bank to engage

The conversation appears in the client’s Search Inbox.

If the conversation is urgent, the system can also send a live alert to the War Room.

This allows the company to react quickly before the opportunity or risk passes.

---

## 6. Historical Search Workflow

Historical search is used for research and strategy.

Instead of monitoring only new conversations, the client can search past conversations over a selected period.

Example questions:

- What did people say about mobile banking last year?
- What were the biggest complaints about our competitor?
- What product problems are customers talking about most?
- Which platforms had the loudest conversations?
- When did negative sentiment peak?

The system returns:

- Total mentions
- Sentiment breakdown
- Platform breakdown
- Volume over time
- Top conversations
- Topic clusters
- Common questions
- Pain points
- Opportunities
- Plain-English insights summary

This is useful for pitches, research, campaign planning, crisis analysis, and competitor intelligence.

---

## 7. Search Inbox Workflow

The Search Inbox is where the client reviews conversations found by the system.

Each conversation card shows:

- Platform
- Author
- Matched keyword
- Message preview
- Sentiment
- Intent
- Urgency
- Engagement metrics
- Date
- Link to original conversation

The client can filter conversations by:

- Keyword group
- Platform
- Date range
- Sentiment
- Intent
- Urgency

When the client finds a useful conversation, they can open it and ask the system to generate reply options.

---

## 8. Reply Assistance Workflow

When a company wants to respond to a conversation, the system can suggest replies in the brand’s tone of voice.

Example:

A person posts:

> “This bank app keeps failing when I need it most.”

The system may suggest:

> “Hi, sorry you’re dealing with that. If you’re looking for a more reliable banking experience, we’d be happy to help you get started.”

The client can:

- Use the reply as-is
- Edit it
- Copy it manually
- Approve it for publishing where platform connection allows

This helps teams respond faster while still keeping human control.

---

## 9. Own-Post Reply Workflow

Social listening is for public keyword conversations across the internet.

Own-post reply management is different.

Own-post reply management handles comments on the company’s own social media posts.

Workflow:

1. The company connects its social accounts.
2. The system receives or syncs comments from the company’s posts.
3. The system classifies the comments.
4. AI generates reply options.
5. Safe replies can be approved or sent.
6. Risky replies can be placed in an approval queue.
7. Activity is tracked for reporting.

This helps customer service, social media, and marketing teams manage conversations at scale.

## 11. War Room Workflow

The War Room is the platform’s alert and risk-monitoring area.

It helps companies spot:

- Sudden negative sentiment
- High urgency complaints
- Crisis keywords
- Spikes in conversation volume

Example alert:

> Negative mentions about “delivery delay” increased sharply today. Status: AMBER. Recommended action: respond quickly and prepare public clarification.

The War Room gives teams a quick view of whether things are normal, concerning, urgent, or critical.

## 13. How Companies Connect Social Media Accounts

Clients should not normally paste API keys into the system.

Instead, they connect accounts using official social media login flows.

The client experience should be:

1. The client opens Settings.
2. They go to Connected Accounts.
3. They click Connect Instagram, Facebook, X, or TikTok.
4. They are redirected to the official platform login page.
5. They approve access.
6. They return to Social Emblue AI.
7. The account shows as connected.

This is safer and easier than asking clients to handle API keys manually.

The client never sees your master platform keys.

Your platform owns the main developer app credentials. The client only grants permission for their own account or page.

---

## 14. What API Keys Are Needed

There are two categories of keys.

### Platform Owner Keys

These belong to Social Emblue AI, not the client.

They allow the system to operate.

Examples:

- AI provider key
- Database connection
- Social platform developer app credentials
- Public search provider key
- Email provider key
- Image storage key

These should be stored securely by the platform owner.

Clients should not see or control them.

### Client Connection Tokens

These are created when a client connects their own social media account.

The client does not manually copy them.

They are issued by the social platform after the client approves access.

The system stores them securely and uses them only for that client’s workspace.

## 15. How Public Social Listening Is Different

Public keyword listening does not always require the client to connect their own social media accounts.

For public keyword monitoring, the platform uses its own search infrastructure.

The client only provides:

- Keywords
- Platforms
- Date range
- Alert settings

Example:

**Keywords:** Failed transaction, cheap flights Lagos, where to buy iPhone Lagos  
**Platforms:** X, Reddit, YouTube  
**Mode:** Real-time and historical

The system then searches public conversations and returns relevant results.
